Investment Summary

PetroChina (0857.HK) — Integrated oil giant with diversified upstream + downstream operations. Revenue 7x larger than CNOOC. Higher dividend yield (6.26%) and cheaper on P/B (0.89x). However, lower ROE (9.6%) and net margins (5.5%) due to heavy downstream/chemical exposure. Revenue/profit declining due to lower oil prices. Analyst target HK$12.17 (+29% upside). Better for value/income investors.

CNOOC (0883.HK) — Upstream pure-play with industry-leading cost structure ($27.90/boe). Higher ROE (19%), net margins (30.7%), and faster production growth (+7.4%). Record reserves of 7,773M boe. More leveraged to oil price recovery. Capex of RMB 112-122B targeting 780-800M boe in 2026. Lower dividend yield (5.1%) but higher earnings quality. Better for growth + quality investors.

Oil Price Context: Brent averaged $68.2 in 2025 (-15% YoY), pressuring both. A recovery to $75+ would significantly benefit CNOOC (upstream leverage), while PetroChina's downstream/refining acts as a partial hedge. Both offer defensive high-dividend exposure in the HK market.
